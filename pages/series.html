Book Series

Applied Linguistics Press currently publishes books in three distinct series, in addition to the general catalog.

Got an idea for a new book series? ALP would love to hear about it! Series proposals can address any area within the broad field of applied linguistics. You can download and fill out the proposal template here, or feel free to get in touch with Luke via email to discuss before submitting.

1. Syllabi in Applied Linguistics

Series Editors: Benjamin Kremmel & Paula Winke

This series publishes course syllabi and instructional resources for applied linguistics courses at undergraduate and graduate levels. The goal is to make high-quality course materials freely available to instructors worldwide, supporting the teaching of applied linguistics across diverse institutional contexts.

Visit the series page →

2. Affect and Care in English Language Education

Series Editors: Chunhong Liu, Bong-gi Sohn, & Angel Lin

This series publishes works exploring the emotional, relational, and humanistic dimensions of language teaching and learning. Books in this series attend to the affective experiences of both teachers and learners, and to the ethics of care in language education contexts.

Visit the series page →

3. Corpus Linguistics and Technology-Mediated Language Education in the AI Era

Series Editors: Pascual Pérez-Paredes & Qing Ma

This series publishes research and pedagogy at the intersection of corpus linguistics, educational technology, and artificial intelligence. It addresses how digital tools and AI are transforming the teaching and learning of language, and how corpus methods can inform these developments.
